Our Story

Where intelligence and fashion meet.

Founded by De Montfort University’s Fashion Management with Marketing alumni, 5Ws of Fashion is a research and events management consultancy rooted in the business of fashion, bridging the gap between academic insight and real-world practice. We don’t just host events - we create environments for immersive learning experiences, foundations for unpopular fashion perspectives to be expressed, genuine alignment with sustainable practice can be fostered, and where Leicester is championed as the British fashion hub where our story began. In the A for Access and Equity - change fashion expertise to fashion insights and modules without extortionate fees - because expertise is kinda deep and can open us up to claims if something goes wrong, and if we are trying to have university partners, we wouldn't want to look like we are trying to steal their business too.Founded by Rumbie Makonise after recognising the gap between fashion management theory and real-world practice, 5 Ws of Fashion is a research and events talent agency that gives conscious consumers, brands, and the next generation of fashion professionals a space to meet, learn, and lead.

We don't just host events. We create environments where unpopular opinions can be spoken freely, where businesses align authentically with sustainable values, and where Leicester reclaims its rightful place at the forefront of British fashion.

Mine Yeter is a Turkish designer raised in England, shaped by the richness of two cultures that inform both her creative and professional identity. Born in Gaziantep, a city renowned for its generational craftsmanship, she grew up surrounded by artisans whose skills in coppersmithing, leather shoemaking, and woodworking were passed down through family-run bazaars. This early exposure to heritage craft instilled in her a deep respect for making, materiality, and tradition. Raised within the structure and multicultural landscape of British society, Mine developed a balanced perspective that blends discipline with generosity, values rooted equally in her Turkish heritage and British upbringing. This duality now defines her design philosophy: honouring tradition while responding to contemporary cultural shifts. In 2025, she completed a Master of Arts in Fashion Management with Marketing, dedicating her research to the leather industry and examining Generation Z’s capacity to revive traditional craftsmanship within a technology-driven era. Her work critically explores the compatibility between heritage leathercraft and modern design thinking, with research soon to be published on young designers and the leathercraft sector. Mine currently contributes as a designer to the DMU Museum’s Artefacts Live exhibition project while working as a Junior Leather Goods Sample Maker in Rutland, Leicestershire, for one of the UK’s leading belt suppliers, producing for high-street and designer brands. Specialising in leather goods design and production, she bridges research and hands-on craft, ensuring heritage skills evolve through informed, contemporary execution.

Rumbie is a founder and fashion entrepreneur, born in Zimbabwe and raised in England. She is a published children’s author and researcher, holding a First-Class Law degree and a Master of Arts in Fashion Management with Marketing. She currently works in London as a Sales and Marketing Specialist with national clients across the food, sustainability, and energy sectors. Her interest in fashion began in her teenage years. Although accepted into fashion design school, she chose to pursue law due to concerns about the fashion industry’s inaccessibility at the time — a decision that redirected her passions towards the business of fashion. Her entrepreneurial spirit was evident early on. In high school, she ran a cookies and doughnuts business during break times, earning the nickname “the businesswoman,” alongside being voted the funniest girl in her year group. During her legal studies, Rumbie distinguished herself as driven and strategic. She was awarded Best Advocate and the winner of the Northants and Bucks Junior Lawyers Division Moot Competition in 2021. She also won a legal debate competition, competed in an online negotiation competition during the pandemic, and represented her host Swiss university in the international European Law Moot Court competition in Barcelona. These experiences sharpened her advocacy, research, teamwork, leadership, and analytical skills, enabling her to take on positions including Equality, Diversity and Inclusion Representative for the Methodist Church in Great Britain and De Montfort University School of Fashion and Textiles, respectively. While living in Switzerland, she founded an accessory brand that deepened her interest in commercial law and the hard luxury market, ultimately inspiring her transition to fashion school. Through the venture, she gained experience in brand, client, and project management, international stakeholder relations, contract drafting and IP registration, digital marketing, consumer research, and industry events including Milan Fashion Week production assistance. Rumbie later negotiated a research brief with Lumbers Jewellers, delivering strategic insights into the next-generation consumer perceptions of hard luxury products. This collaboration inspired the concept of the *5 Ws of Fashion*.
