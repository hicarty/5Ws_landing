Our Story

Where intelligence and fashion meet.

Founded by De Montfort University’s Fashion Management with Marketing alumni, 5Ws of Fashion is a research and events management agency rooted in the business of fashion, bridging the gap between academic insight and real-world practice.

We don’t just host events - we create environments for immersive learning experiences, foundations for unpopular fashion perspectives to be expressed, genuine alignment with sustainable practice can be fostered, and where Leicester is championed as the British fashion hub where our story began.

Meet the team

Adufe
Adufe Lagos
Adufe is a Nigerian fashion designer and entrepreneur whose work explores the intersection of African creativity, cultural identity, and global fashion strategy. Born and raised in Lagos, Nigeria, one of Africa’s most influential fashion capitals, her perspective is shaped by the city’s vibrant style culture and its spirit of bold self-expression. In 2024, she relocated to the United Kingdom to pursue a Master’s degree in Fashion Management with Marketing at De Montfort University in Leicester, further developing her understanding of the business and strategic dynamics shaping the global fashion industry. She is currently based in Belfast. Growing up with parents known for their expressive and maximalist approach to style, Adufe developed an early appreciation for fashion as a powerful form of identity and storytelling. Inspired by this environment and Lagos’ dynamic fashion scene, she founded her fashion brand Adufe Lagos in 2020 as a passion project celebrating confident aesthetics and contemporary interpretations of African style. Through her entrepreneurial journey, Adufe has gained experience in creative direction, brand development, and fashion business operations while building her own label. Her work increasingly focuses on how emerging designers from culturally rich yet underrepresented fashion regions can successfully position themselves within international markets. In 2025, she collaborated with a team in partnership with Lumbers on a project highlighting designers from globally underrepresented fashion regions, exploring the structural challenges many encounter when entering international fashion spaces. The exhibition brought together creative voices from diverse cultural backgrounds. This work complemented her postgraduate research examining how fashion brands from emerging markets, especially African markets can navigate global fashion systems while overcoming barriers to international growth. Through her work, Adufe advocates for greater visibility, strategic support, and sustainable opportunities for designers from emerging fashion regions within the global fashion ecosystem.
Manali
Ruii Clothing
Manali Kamble is a multidisciplinary fashion and brand professional whose work spans fashion design, visual branding, and creative direction. With over eight years of experience across the creative industries, her practice is shaped by a deep connection to nature and the cultural values rooted in her Indian upbringing. Growing up in an environment where respect for materials and mindful living were part of everyday life, sustainability naturally became an integral part of her creative outlook. Her journey into design began at Bharati Vidyapeeth College of Fine Arts, where she was first introduced to art and visual expression. This foundation helped shape her design sensibilities and led her to pursue a Master’s degree in Fashion Design. Her work during this time explored narrative-driven collections and craftsmanship, eventually leading to the presentation of her collection at London Fashion Week – Fashion Scout, alongside features in several fashion editorials. Driven by a desire to create responsibly, Manali later founded Ruii Clothing, a sustainable childrenswear label in India that combined thoughtful production with contemporary aesthetics. In 2025, she completed a second Master’s degree in Luxury Fashion Brand Management at Nottingham Trent University, expanding her perspective on the strategic and cultural dimensions of the fashion industry. Alongside her design practice, she has gained experience across branding and marketing, contributing to creative projects with brands such as Cadbury at Contract Advertising, as well as Bougainvillea and Kelkar Foods & Fragrances. Today, Manali’s work brings together design, strategy, and storytelling, exploring how thoughtful creativity can shape meaningful and enduring brand experiences.
Mine
DMU Museum
Mine Yeter is a Turkish designer raised in England, shaped by the richness of two cultures that inform both her creative and professional identity. Born in Gaziantep, a city renowned for its generational craftsmanship, she grew up surrounded by artisans whose skills in coppersmithing, leather shoemaking, and woodworking were passed down through family-run bazaars. This early exposure to heritage craft instilled in her a deep respect for making, materiality, and tradition. Raised within the structure and multicultural landscape of British society, Mine developed a balanced perspective that blends discipline with generosity, values rooted equally in her Turkish heritage and British upbringing. This duality now defines her design philosophy: honouring tradition while responding to contemporary cultural shifts. In 2025, she completed a Master of Arts in Fashion Management with Marketing, dedicating her research to the leather industry and examining Generation Z’s capacity to revive traditional craftsmanship within a technology-driven era. Her work critically explores the compatibility between heritage leathercraft and modern design thinking, with research soon to be published on young designers and the leathercraft sector. Mine currently contributes as a designer to the DMU Museum’s Artefacts Live exhibition project while working as a Junior Leather Goods Sample Maker in Rutland, Leicestershire, for one of the UK’s leading belt suppliers, producing for high-street and designer brands. Specialising in leather goods design and production, she bridges research and hands-on craft, ensuring heritage skills evolve through informed, contemporary execution.
Rumbie
14fashion Group
Rumbie is a founder and fashion entrepreneur, born in Zimbabwe and raised in England. She is a published children’s author and researcher, holding a First-Class Law degree and a Master of Arts in Fashion Management with Marketing. She currently works in London as a Sales and Marketing Specialist with national clients across the food, sustainability, and energy sectors. Her interest in fashion began in her teenage years. Although accepted into fashion design school, she chose to pursue law due to concerns about the fashion industry’s inaccessibility at the time — a decision that redirected her passions towards the business of fashion. Her entrepreneurial spirit was evident early on. In high school, she ran a cookies and doughnuts business during break times, earning the nickname “the businesswoman,” alongside being voted the funniest girl in her year group. During her legal studies, Rumbie distinguished herself as driven and strategic. She was awarded Best Advocate and the winner of the Northants and Bucks Junior Lawyers Division Moot Competition in 2021. She also won a legal debate competition, competed in an online negotiation competition during the pandemic, and represented her host Swiss university in the international European Law Moot Court competition in Barcelona. These experiences sharpened her advocacy, research, teamwork, leadership, and analytical skills, enabling her to take on positions including Equality, Diversity and Inclusion Representative for the Methodist Church in Great Britain and De Montfort University School of Fashion and Textiles, respectively. While living in Switzerland, she founded an accessory brand that deepened her interest in commercial law and the hard luxury market, ultimately inspiring her transition to fashion school. Through the venture, she gained experience in brand, client, and project management, international stakeholder relations, contract drafting and IP registration, digital marketing, consumer research, and industry events including Milan Fashion Week production assistance. Rumbie later negotiated a research brief with Lumbers Jewellers, delivering strategic insights into the next-generation consumer perceptions of hard luxury products. This collaboration inspired the concept of the 5 Ws of Fashion.
Samia
Academic & Master
Samia Irshad, born in Qatar and raised in Abu Dhabi, is an interdisciplinary researcher and creative strategist. Her upbringing in the Gulf, a region where accelerated urban development, luxury enterprise, and technological ambition coexist, shaped her understanding of how prestige, innovation, and cultural identity operate within global markets. This international foundation continues to inform her academic and creative work, grounding her perspective in cross-cultural fluency and a nuanced awareness of evolving luxury economies. Driven by a desire to explore the creative dimensions of fashion and innovation, she completed a Master of Arts in Fashion Management and Marketing at De Montfort University, where her research critically examined how luxury fashion brands construct and communicate ethical identity, through immersive technologies to reshape symbolic value, exclusivity, and consumer trust. Her work positioned digital innovation not simply as a marketing tool, but as a structural force transforming how value and authenticity are negotiated in prestige markets. Going back to her roots, she is currently undertaking a PhD that investigates the ethical and socio-technical implications of emerging technologies across high-value sectors of real estate and urban development. Her doctoral research investigates the impact of property technologies and digitally mediated infrastructures on systems of value and governance in rapidly developing markets. A clear intellectual continuity connects both degrees: a sustained focus on how digital transformation reconfigures value creation, legitimacy, and identity across prestige-driven industries. Alongside her academic research, she draws on her background in PR and integrated marketing to volunteer as a creative director for societies and fashion brands, leading storytelling and strategic direction through a research-informed lens. Defined by strategic storytelling, ethical intelligence, and technological fluency, Samia aims to combine academic depth with marketing expertise to shape luxury narratives that are culturally attuned, digitally literate, and built for enduring relevance
Wijdane
Academic
Wijdane grew up in a Moroccan environment where creativity and cultural expression were deeply valued, shaping her sense of identity from an early age. Encouraged by her parents' love for the arts, she developed a passion for visual culture, winning several art competitions that opened doors to an internship at a local textile institute. This early hands-on experience sparked her shift from a rigorous academic path in the hard sciences - where she had developed discipline and analytical thinking toward the arts and heritage crafts. Her multilingual background further enriched her understanding of culture, communication, and visual expressions, allowing her to approach fashion through diverse cultural and conceptual lenses. These formative experiences deeply influenced her academic focus during postgraduate studies in the UK, where she began weaving together cultural heritage, business strategy, and sustainability within the fashion sector. Her academic and Industry training in textile production, marketing and the history of traditional dress has shaped her research focus on craft traditions, including Amazigh textiles - the Indigenous North African communities whose name means "free people." She explores how artisanal knowledge, materiality, identity, and historical context intersect with digital representation by designing digital platforms and technology-driven strategies that support community empowerment, alongside branding and marketing within contemporary fashion markets. Through design practice, she has gained experience in qualitative research, interview-based fieldwork, and data analysis, applying cultural knowledge to sustainable design and heritage innovation. Her work also examines the relationship between business psychology, marketing, and sustainability, particularly how authenticity and cultural narratives shape consumer perception and brand engagement. She is interested in how museums and cultural institutions connect textile traditions with contemporary audiences, with an emphasis on sensory experiences, contributing to sustainable fashion discourse.
Namruthaa
Entrepreneur
Namruthaa Dhanashekar is an entrepreneur and fashion professional from Bangalore, widely known as the Silicon Valley of India which is known for it’s innovative and tech forward startups. Growing up in one of India’s most diverse and vibrant city, she was surrounded by a rich blend of traditions, languages, and artistic influences that shaped her perspective on style and creativity. Her roots in the fashion and textile world run deep,she comes from a family with a history in the garment industry that began with trading and gradually evolved into a thriving textile enterprise. Over two generations, her family established Kamalam Handlooms, building a legacy in handloom textiles while also expanding into yarn manufacturing and dyeing. This environment immersed her in the craftsmanship, processes, and cultural significance behind clothing from a young age, naturally fostering her interest in fashion and clothing. Inspired by this heritage, Namruthaa pursued formal education in the field, earning a Bachelor of Science in Fashion Design along with a diploma in Fashion Styling. Wanting to combine creativity with business insight, she later completed a Master’s degree in Fashion Management and Marketing at De Montfort University equipping herself with the entrepreneurial knowledge needed to build and grow a brand. Today, Namruthaa is an entrepreneur working on launching her own fashion brand. Deeply passionate about thrifting and vintage aesthetics, she aims to incorporate these influences and circularity into her work by focusing on heirloom-inspired pieces and timeless vintage charm. Through her brand, she hopes to celebrate heritage, sustainability, and the enduring beauty of garments that carry stories across generations.
